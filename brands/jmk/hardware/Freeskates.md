# JMK Freeskate Hardware

![](/jmk/freeskate-variant-cottoncandy.png)

The flagship [JMK Freeskate](https://usa.jmkride.com/products/jmkride-custom-freeskates) setup consists of two completely independent, unattached skates. Each individual skate is modular and constructed from a standard set of interchangeable components.

## Part Overview

Every skate is built using the following core parts:

**Decks:** The top platform where the rider's foot rests.

**Trucks:** The metal frame housing the wheels and anchoring them to the deck.

**Wheels:** Two wheels per skate, designed for carving and maintaining momentum. you can choose beetween [proformance and classic wheels](https://www.jmkride.com/post/jmkride-wheels-comparison)

[**Custom Bearings**](https://usa.jmkride.com/products/jmkride-bearings)**:** Specialized custom bearings that cant simply be replaced by standard bearings.

**Grip Tape:** Applied to the deck surface to ensure secure, slip-resistant foot placement.

**Hardware:** Screws and mounting components holding the entire assembly together.

## Customization & Styling

Depending on the specific package or version purchased, riders can customize the color of the **grip tape, deck, wheels, and trucks** individually for each of the two skates. This allows for highly personalized, asymmetrical colorways.

## Additional info

**Self-Assembly:** Out of the box, JMK setups do not arrive pre-assembled. Riders receive the raw components and must assemble the skates themselves. This ensures that every skater understands the structural anatomy of their hardware from day one.

**Fully Modular Replacement:** Every single component of a JMK freeskate can be purchased individually as a standalone accessory or replacement part. This eliminates the need to buy a whole new set if a single piece wears out and makes custom hybrid setups easy to build.
